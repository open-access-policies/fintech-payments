# Money Movement and Funds Transfer Policy (FIN-POL-004)

## 1. Objective

This policy establishes **[Company Name]**'s requirements for the secure, compliant movement of funds including wire transfers, ACH transactions, and other electronic fund transfers. It ensures compliance with the Bank Secrecy Act, Electronic Fund Transfer Act (Regulation E), funds transfer rules (UCC Article 4A), Nacha Operating Rules, and state money transmitter licensing requirements.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All wire transfers (domestic and international)
- All ACH transactions (credits and debits)
- All internal fund movements
- Real-time payment (RTP) transactions
- Card-based fund transfers
- All employees involved in funds transfer operations
- All third-party payment processors and correspondents

## 3. Policy

**[Company Name]** shall maintain comprehensive controls over all funds movement to ensure accuracy, security, regulatory compliance, and protection against fraud and financial crime.

### 3.1 Funds Transfer Authorization

All fund transfers shall be properly authorized before execution.

**Authorization Requirements:**
- Originator identity verified before initiating transfer
- Transfer instructions authenticated
- Dual approval for high-value transfers above **[$Amount, e.g., $50,000]**
- Customer authorization verified for account debits

**Authorization Methods:**
- Digital signature or secure authentication for electronic requests
- Voice callback verification for phone requests above threshold
- Dual control for internal fund movements
- Written authorization retained for all transfers

**Standing Instructions:**
- Standing transfer instructions documented and approved
- Periodic review of standing instructions
- Changes to standing instructions require re-authorization

### 3.2 Wire Transfer Requirements

Wire transfers shall comply with applicable regulations and security standards.

**Domestic Wire Transfers:**
- Collect complete originator and beneficiary information
- Verify against sanctions lists before processing
- Retain records per BSA requirements
- Same-day processing as applicable

**International Wire Transfers:**
- Complete FATF Travel Rule information included
- OFAC screening before processing
- Country risk assessment applied
- Correspondent bank relationships maintained

**Travel Rule Compliance:**
- Include originator name, address, and account number
- Include beneficiary name and account number
- Include intermediary information as applicable
- Retain transmittal order records

**Wire Transfer Security:**
- Secure communication channels (SWIFT, Fedwire)
- Encryption for wire instructions
- Segregation of duties (initiation, approval, release)
- Daily reconciliation of wire activity

### 3.3 ACH Transaction Requirements

ACH transactions shall comply with Nacha Operating Rules and applicable regulations.

**Origination Requirements:**
- Authorization obtained before originating ACH debits
- Authorization retained per Nacha rules
- Proper SEC codes used for transaction type
- Company identification properly registered

**ACH Authorization Types:**
- Written authorization (WEB, TEL, PPD, etc.)
- Consumer authorizations per Regulation E
- Corporate authorizations per agreement
- Prenotification where required

**ACH Returns and Disputes:**
- Process returns within required timeframes
- Investigate unauthorized transaction claims per Regulation E
- Maintain return rate within Nacha thresholds
- Monitor for suspicious return patterns

**ACH Security:**
- ACH origination controls
- Account validation before debiting
- Fraud monitoring for ACH activity
- Protection of ACH file transmission

### 3.4 Transaction Monitoring and Limits

Funds transfers shall be monitored and subject to appropriate limits.

**Transaction Limits:**
- Daily limits per customer/account
- Per-transaction limits based on product/risk
- Velocity limits for transaction frequency
- Cumulative limits as appropriate

**Limit Management:**
- Limits documented and approved
- Limit increases require additional approval
- High-limit transactions may require additional verification
- Limits reviewed periodically

**Monitoring:**
- Real-time monitoring for high-risk patterns
- Velocity monitoring
- Geographic anomaly detection
- Amount pattern analysis
- Integration with AML transaction monitoring

### 3.5 Recordkeeping Requirements

Complete records shall be maintained for all funds transfers.

**Record Content:**
- Originator information
- Beneficiary information
- Amount and currency
- Date and time
- Authorization documentation
- Intermediary information (wires)

**Retention Periods:**
- Wire transfer records: 5 years
- ACH records: 5 years (6 years for certain disputes)
- Authorization records: Duration of authorization plus 2 years (ACH) or 5 years (wire)

**Retrievability:**
- Records must be retrievable within reasonable timeframe
- Electronic records backed up appropriately
- Records available for regulatory examination

### 3.6 Correspondent Banking Relationships

Correspondent relationships shall be properly managed.

**Due Diligence:**
- Due diligence on correspondent banks
- Understand correspondent's AML program
- Prohibitions per Section 311 designations
- Periodic review of correspondent relationships

**Prohibited Relationships:**
- No correspondent relationships with shell banks
- No payable-through accounts without appropriate controls
- No relationships with Section 311 designated entities

**Nested Relationships:**
- Policies on downstream correspondents
- Understand correspondent's customer base
- Enhanced monitoring for high-risk corridors

### 3.7 Regulatory Reporting

Required regulatory reports shall be filed for funds transfers.

**Currency Transaction Reports:**
- File CTR for currency transactions over $10,000
- Aggregate multiple transactions as required
- File within 15 days

**Suspicious Activity Reports:**
- Monitor transfers for suspicious patterns
- File SARs for suspicious funds movements
- Wire transfers are often involved in suspicious activity

**International Wire Transfer Reporting:**
- Report international wire transfers as required
- Maintain required information for reporting

### 3.8 Fraud Prevention

Controls shall be implemented to prevent funds transfer fraud.

**Fraud Prevention Measures:**
- Multi-factor authentication for transfer initiation
- Out-of-band verification for large or unusual transfers
- Payee verification and confirmation
- Transaction anomaly detection

**Business Email Compromise (BEC):**
- Awareness training for BEC schemes
- Verification procedures for payment instructions
- Callback verification using known numbers
- Email authentication controls

**Customer Protections:**
- Fraud monitoring alerts
- Transaction limits
- Customer notification of large transfers
- Prompt investigation of customer complaints

### 3.9 Error Resolution

Funds transfer errors shall be resolved promptly.

**Consumer Transactions (Regulation E):**
- Investigate within 10 business days (20 for new accounts)
- Provisional credit as required
- Correct errors promptly
- Written determination provided

**Commercial Transactions:**
- Handle per agreement terms
- Prompt investigation of claimed errors
- Cooperation with beneficiary bank
- Documentation of resolution

**Error Types:**
- Incorrect amount
- Incorrect beneficiary
- Duplicate transaction
- Unauthorized transaction

### 3.10 State Money Transmitter Compliance

Money transmission activities shall comply with applicable state licensing requirements.

**Licensing:**
- Maintain required state money transmitter licenses
- Meet net worth and bonding requirements
- File required reports with state regulators
- Renew licenses timely

**Examination:**
- Cooperate with state examinations
- Maintain required records
- Implement examination findings

**Permitted Activities:**
- Operate only within scope of licenses held
- Monitor for regulatory changes affecting licensing

## 5. Definitions

| **Term** | **Definition** |
|---|---|
| **ACH (Automated Clearing House)** | An electronic network for financial transactions in the United States. |
| **Correspondent Bank** | A bank that provides services on behalf of another bank, typically in a different jurisdiction. |
| **Nacha** | The organization that governs and administers the ACH Network. |
| **Originator** | The entity that initiates a funds transfer instruction. |
| **Payable-Through Account** | An account maintained at a U.S. bank that allows foreign bank customers direct access. |
| **SEC Code** | Standard Entry Class code identifying the type of ACH transaction. |
| **Travel Rule** | Requirement to include and transmit originator and beneficiary information with funds transfers. |
| **Wire Transfer** | An electronic transfer of funds through a network like SWIFT or Fedwire. |
