# OFAC Sanctions Compliance Policy (FIN-POL-003)

## 1. Objective

This policy establishes **[Company Name]**'s requirements for compliance with economic and trade sanctions administered by the Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC), ensuring that the company does not engage in prohibited transactions with sanctioned countries, entities, or individuals.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All **[Company Name]** business activities and transactions
- All customers, vendors, partners, and counterparties
- All products and services offered by **[Company Name]**
- All employees, contractors, and agents of **[Company Name]**
- All jurisdictions where **[Company Name]** operates

## 3. Policy

**[Company Name]** shall maintain a comprehensive OFAC sanctions compliance program to prevent prohibited transactions and ensure compliance with all applicable sanctions laws and regulations.

### 3.1 OFAC Compliance Framework

**[Company Name]** shall maintain a risk-based sanctions compliance program.

**Program Elements:**
- Management commitment and oversight
- Sanctions risk assessment
- Internal controls (policies, procedures, and systems)
- Testing and auditing
- Training

**Prohibited Activities:**
- Transactions with Specially Designated Nationals (SDNs)
- Transactions with blocked persons or entities
- Transactions involving sanctioned countries or regions
- Evading or attempting to evade sanctions requirements
- Facilitating prohibited transactions for others

**Strict Liability:**
- OFAC sanctions are strict liability - intent is not required for a violation
- All employees must exercise diligence to prevent prohibited transactions

### 3.2 Sanctions Screening

All parties to transactions shall be screened against OFAC sanctions lists.

**Screening Requirements:**
- Screen customers at onboarding
- Screen counterparties before transactions
- Screen vendors and partners before engagement
- Screen periodically for ongoing relationships
- Screen when lists are updated

**Lists Screened:**
- OFAC Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons (SDN) List
- OFAC Consolidated Sanctions List (including sectoral sanctions)
- Other OFAC lists as applicable
- Additional lists based on risk assessment (UN, EU, etc.)

**Screening Timing:**
- New customers: Before account opening
- Existing customers: Upon list updates and periodically
- Transactions: Before processing for high-risk transactions
- Wire transfers: Before or during processing

### 3.3 Match Handling and Escalation

Potential sanctions matches shall be handled appropriately.

**Initial Match Review:**
- Trained staff review potential matches
- Determine if match is true positive or false positive
- Use available information to disposition
- Document match review and decision

**True Match Response:**
- Stop transaction immediately
- Do not notify the customer (no tipping)
- Block account/funds as required
- Escalate to BSA/AML Officer immediately
- Report to OFAC within required timeframe

**False Positive Documentation:**
- Document reason for false positive determination
- Retain documentation for audit purposes
- Consider adding to internal "do not screen" list if appropriate

### 3.4 Blocked and Rejected Transactions

Blocked and rejected transactions shall be handled per OFAC requirements.

**Blocked Transactions:**
- Property of SDNs must be blocked (frozen)
- Blocked funds held in an interest-bearing account
- Annual reports filed with OFAC
- Released only with OFAC license

**Rejected Transactions:**
- Transactions involving prohibited countries may be rejected
- Documentation of rejection maintained
- Reports filed with OFAC as required

**Reporting:**
- Blocking reports filed within 10 business days
- Rejected transaction reports filed within 10 business days
- Annual reports of blocked property filed by September 30

### 3.5 Sanctions Programs

**[Company Name]** shall comply with all applicable OFAC sanctions programs.

**Comprehensive Sanctions:**
- Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Syria, Crimea region
- Broadly prohibit transactions with these jurisdictions
- Minimal or no permitted activities

**List-Based Programs:**
- Target specific individuals and entities
- Require screening and blocking of designated parties
- Various programs (narcotics, terrorism, cyber, etc.)

**Sectoral Sanctions:**
- Target specific sectors of certain countries
- May allow some transactions while prohibiting others
- Require careful analysis of permitted activities

**Secondary Sanctions:**
- Consider secondary sanctions implications
- Non-U.S. persons may face restrictions for certain activities
- Assess counterparty exposure to secondary sanctions

### 3.6 Geographic and Transaction Controls

Controls shall be implemented based on country and transaction risk.

**Prohibited Countries/Regions:**
- Block transactions to/from comprehensively sanctioned countries
- Implement IP blocking for online services if applicable
- Review customer/transaction country information

**High-Risk Countries:**
- Enhanced due diligence for transactions involving high-risk countries
- Additional approval requirements
- Enhanced monitoring

**Transaction Controls:**
- Wire transfer message screening
- Payment blocking rules
- Trade finance review

### 3.7 OFAC Licensing

OFAC licenses may authorize otherwise prohibited activities.

**License Types:**
- General licenses: Authorize categories of transactions
- Specific licenses: Authorize specific transactions upon application

**License Compliance:**
- Activities conducted under license must comply with license terms
- Document compliance with license conditions
- Report as required by license

**License Application:**
- BSA/AML Officer coordinates license applications
- Legal counsel review before submission
- Documentation of application and response

### 3.8 Training

All relevant personnel shall receive OFAC sanctions training.

**Training Requirements:**
- All employees: Basic sanctions awareness
- Compliance and operations staff: Detailed sanctions training
- Senior management: Oversight responsibilities
- Training upon hire and annually thereafter

**Training Content:**
- Overview of OFAC and sanctions programs
- **[Company Name]** policies and procedures
- Red flags for sanctions evasion
- Screening and escalation procedures
- Consequences of violations

### 3.9 Record Retention

Records shall be retained per OFAC requirements.

**Retention Requirements:**
- All records related to blocked or rejected transactions: 5 years
- Screening records: 5 years
- Training records: 5 years after employment
- License documentation: 5 years from expiration

### 3.10 Enforcement and Penalties

Sanctions violations carry significant penalties.

**Potential Consequences:**
- Civil penalties up to $311,562+ per violation (adjusted annually)
- Criminal penalties for willful violations
- Reputational damage
- Regulatory enforcement actions

**Voluntary Self-Disclosure:**
- Violations should be assessed for voluntary disclosure to OFAC
- Self-disclosure may reduce penalties significantly
- Legal counsel consulted before disclosure

**Internal Violations:**
- Employees who violate sanctions policy subject to disciplinary action
- Violations reported to BSA/AML Officer
- Root cause analysis and remediation

## 5. Definitions

| **Term** | **Definition** |
|---|---|
| **Blocked Property** | Property in which an SDN or other blocked person has an interest, which must be frozen and reported to OFAC. |
| **Comprehensive Sanctions** | Sanctions that broadly prohibit transactions with a particular country or region. |
| **OFAC (Office of Foreign Assets Control)** | U.S. Treasury agency that administers and enforces economic and trade sanctions. |
| **SDN (Specially Designated National)** | An individual or entity designated by OFAC whose assets must be blocked and with whom transactions are prohibited. |
| **Sectoral Sanctions** | Sanctions targeting specific sectors of a country's economy rather than blocking all transactions. |
| **50% Rule** | Entities owned 50% or more by blocked persons are also blocked, even if not listed. |

## 6. Responsibilities

| **Role** | **Responsibility** |
|---|---|
| **BSA/AML Officer** | Oversee OFAC compliance program. Approve match dispositions. Coordinate with OFAC. File required reports. |
| **Compliance Team** | Conduct screening. Review matches. Document dispositions. Maintain screening systems. |
| **Operations Team** | Implement transaction controls. Escalate potential matches. Process blocking actions. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Advise on sanctions interpretation. Review license applications. Guide enforcement response. |
| **IT Team** | Maintain screening systems. Implement technical controls. Support list updates. |
| **All Employees** | Report suspicious activity. Complete training. Follow sanctions procedures. |
