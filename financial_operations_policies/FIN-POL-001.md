# Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Policy (FIN-POL-001)

## 1. Objective

This policy establishes **[Company Name]**'s Anti-Money Laundering (AML) program to detect, prevent, and report money laundering, terrorist financing, and other financial crimes in compliance with the Bank Secrecy Act (BSA), USA PATRIOT Act, and FinCEN regulations. The policy implements the BSA Five Pillars framework to ensure a comprehensive and effective AML compliance program.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All **[Company Name]** business operations involving financial transactions
- All products and services offered by **[Company Name]**
- All employees, contractors, and third parties involved in financial transactions or compliance activities
- All customers, accounts, and transactions
- All jurisdictions where **[Company Name]** operates

## 3. Policy

**[Company Name]** is committed to preventing the use of its products and services for money laundering, terrorist financing, or other illicit financial activities through a robust AML program based on the BSA Five Pillars.

### 3.1 BSA Five Pillars Framework

**[Company Name]**'s AML program shall be based on the five pillars required by the Bank Secrecy Act:

**Pillar 1: Internal Controls**
- Policies, procedures, and processes to ensure ongoing compliance
- Transaction monitoring and screening systems
- Record-keeping and documentation standards
- Quality assurance and control mechanisms

**Pillar 2: Independent Testing**
- Annual independent testing of the AML program
- Testing by internal audit or qualified third party
- Scope includes all BSA/AML functions
- Documented findings and remediation tracking

**Pillar 3: Designation of BSA/AML Officer**
- Designated BSA/AML Officer with appropriate authority
- Clear reporting lines to senior management and board
- Adequate resources and support
- Responsibility for day-to-day BSA/AML compliance

**Pillar 4: Training Program**
- BSA/AML training for all relevant personnel
- Role-based training for specialized functions
- Annual refresher training
- Documentation of training completion

**Pillar 5: Customer Due Diligence (CDD)**
- Risk-based customer due diligence procedures
- Enhanced due diligence for high-risk customers
- Ongoing monitoring of customer relationships
- Beneficial ownership identification

### 3.2 BSA/AML Officer

A designated BSA/AML Officer shall have responsibility for the AML program.

**Designation:**
- The **[Role Title, e.g., Chief Compliance Officer]** or designee serves as the BSA/AML Officer
- The BSA/AML Officer is approved by the Board of Directors
- The designation is documented and communicated

**Responsibilities:**
- Day-to-day oversight of the AML program
- Ensuring policies and procedures are current and effective
- Coordinating with regulatory examiners
- Reporting to senior management and board on program status
- Filing Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)
- Managing relationships with law enforcement

**Authority:**
- Authority to escalate issues to the board
- Access to all relevant business information
- Authority to halt suspicious transactions
- Independence to make compliance decisions

### 3.3 Risk Assessment

A comprehensive AML risk assessment shall be conducted to understand and mitigate money laundering risks.

**Risk Assessment Scope:**
- Customer risk (types, geographic locations, industries)
- Product and service risk
- Geographic risk (jurisdictions served)
- Transaction risk (volume, types, channels)
- Third-party risk

**Risk Assessment Process:**
- Conducted at least annually or when significant changes occur
- Documented methodology and results
- Approved by senior management
- Used to inform control design and resource allocation

**Risk Categories:**
- **High Risk:** Enhanced controls, enhanced due diligence, frequent review
- **Medium Risk:** Standard controls, standard due diligence
- **Low Risk:** Simplified due diligence where permitted

### 3.4 Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

Risk-based customer due diligence shall be performed per FIN-POL-002 (Customer Due Diligence Policy).

**CDD Requirements:**
- Customer identification and verification (CIP)
- Beneficial ownership identification (for legal entity customers)
- Understanding the nature and purpose of customer relationships
- Ongoing monitoring of customer relationships

**Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD):**
- Required for high-risk customers
- Required for politically exposed persons (PEPs)
- Required for customers from high-risk jurisdictions
- Additional information gathering and approval requirements

### 3.5 Transaction Monitoring

Transactions shall be monitored to detect suspicious activity.

**Monitoring Program:**
- Automated transaction monitoring system
- Rule-based and behavior-based detection
- Alert generation for review
- Investigation and documentation of alerts

**Monitoring Scope:**
- All customer transactions
- Wire transfers and ACH
- High-value transactions
- Unusual patterns or behaviors
- Structuring indicators

**Alert Disposition:**
- Alerts reviewed by trained analysts
- Investigation documented
- Escalation to BSA/AML Officer when warranted
- SAR filing decision documented

### 3.6 Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR)

Suspicious activity shall be reported to FinCEN per regulatory requirements.

**SAR Filing Requirements:**
- SARs filed within 30 days of detection (60 days if no suspect identified)
- Filed for transactions of $5,000 or more involving suspicious activity
- Filed for patterns of activity indicative of money laundering
- Filed for terrorist financing indicators

**SAR Process:**
- SAR decision made or approved by BSA/AML Officer
- SARs filed through BSA E-Filing system
- SAR records retained for 5 years
- SAR information maintained confidentially

**SAR Confidentiality:**
- SAR filings and related information are confidential
- Disclosure to subjects is prohibited (no tipping)
- Information shared only with authorized parties

Detailed SAR procedures are defined in FIN-PROC-002 (Suspicious Activity Reporting Procedure).

### 3.7 Currency Transaction Reporting (CTR)

Currency transactions over $10,000 shall be reported.

**CTR Requirements:**
- CTRs filed for currency transactions over $10,000
- Multiple transactions aggregated when required
- Filed within 15 days of transaction
- Accurate and complete information

**Exemptions:**
- Exemptions limited to eligible customers
- Exemption eligibility reviewed annually
- Exemption documentation maintained

### 3.8 Record Retention

AML records shall be retained per regulatory requirements.

**Retention Periods:**
- SARs and supporting documentation: 5 years from filing
- CTRs and supporting documentation: 5 years from filing
- CIP records: 5 years after account closure
- Transaction records: 5 years from transaction
- Training records: Duration of employment plus 5 years

**Record Security:**
- AML records protected from unauthorized access
- Electronic records backed up appropriately
- Records retrievable for examination

### 3.9 Training Program

All relevant personnel shall receive BSA/AML training.

**Training Requirements:**
- All employees: General AML awareness training within 30 days of hire
- Frontline staff: Customer-facing AML training
- Compliance staff: Specialized AML training
- Senior management: Board-level AML training
- Annual refresher training for all

**Training Content:**
- Overview of money laundering and terrorist financing
- BSA/AML regulatory requirements
- **[Company Name]** policies and procedures
- Red flag indicators
- Reporting responsibilities
- Consequences of non-compliance

**Training Documentation:**
- Training completion tracked and documented
- Training materials reviewed and updated annually
- Records maintained for examination

### 3.10 Independent Testing

The AML program shall be independently tested annually.

**Testing Requirements:**
- Testing performed by qualified internal audit or third party
- Tester independent of AML program operations
- Testing conducted at least annually
- Risk-based scope covering all BSA/AML functions

**Testing Scope:**
- Effectiveness of policies and procedures
- Transaction monitoring system effectiveness
- SAR decision-making process
- CDD and EDD processes
- Training program effectiveness
- Record retention compliance

**Findings and Remediation:**
- Findings documented and reported to senior management
- Remediation plans developed for identified issues
- Remediation tracked to completion
- Significant findings reported to board

### 3.11 Regulatory Examinations

**[Company Name]** shall cooperate with regulatory examinations.

**Examination Preparedness:**
- BSA/AML Officer coordinates examination response
- Documentation readily available
- Staff prepared to respond to examiner questions
- Examination findings addressed promptly

**Regulatory Relationships:**
- Maintain positive relationships with regulators
- Respond to information requests timely
- Implement examination findings

## 5. Definitions

| **Term** | **Definition** |
|---|---|
| **Bank Secrecy Act (BSA)** | The primary U.S. anti-money laundering law requiring financial institutions to maintain AML programs and file certain reports. |
| **Beneficial Owner** | Each individual who, directly or indirectly, owns 25% or more of a legal entity customer, and any individual with significant responsibility to control or manage the entity. |
| **Currency Transaction Report (CTR)** | A report filed with FinCEN for currency transactions over $10,000. |
| **Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)** | Additional scrutiny and information gathering required for high-risk customers. |
| **Money Laundering** | The process of making illegally obtained money appear legitimate. |
| **Politically Exposed Person (PEP)** | A current or former senior foreign political figure, their immediate family members, and close associates. |
| **Suspicious Activity Report (SAR)** | A report filed with FinCEN when suspicious activity is detected that may indicate money laundering or other crimes. |
| **Structuring** | The act of breaking up transactions to avoid reporting thresholds. |

| **Compliance Team** | Monitor transactions. Investigate alerts. Prepare SARs. Conduct due diligence. |
| **Internal Audit** | Independent testing of AML program. Report findings to board. |
