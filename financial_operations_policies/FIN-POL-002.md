# Customer Due Diligence (KYC/CDD) Policy (FIN-POL-002)

## 1. Objective

This policy establishes **[Company Name]**'s requirements for Customer Due Diligence (CDD), including Customer Identification Program (CIP), Know Your Customer (KYC), and beneficial ownership identification. It ensures compliance with the BSA CDD Final Rule (31 CFR 1010.230), USA PATRIOT Act Section 326, and FinCEN requirements while enabling risk-based customer onboarding and ongoing monitoring.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All new customer account openings
- All existing customer relationships (ongoing monitoring)
- All customer types (individuals, legal entities, trusts)
- All products and services requiring customer accounts
- All jurisdictions where **[Company Name]** operates

## 3. Policy

**[Company Name]** shall implement a risk-based Customer Due Diligence program that identifies and verifies customers, assesses risk, and monitors customer relationships for suspicious activity.

### 3.1 Customer Identification Program (CIP)

All customers shall be identified and verified before account opening.

**Individual Customer Requirements:**
- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Residential address (not a P.O. Box)
- Government-issued identification number (SSN, TIN, or passport number)

**Legal Entity Customer Requirements:**
- Legal name
- Principal place of business address
- Identification number (EIN or equivalent)
- Formation documents (articles of incorporation, partnership agreement)

**Verification Methods:**
- Documentary verification (government-issued ID, corporate documents)
- Non-documentary verification (credit bureau, public records)
- Verification of all required information before account is fully functional
- Records of verification method and results

**CIP Exceptions:**
- Documented exceptions only when legally permitted
- Exception approval by designated personnel
- Enhanced monitoring for exception cases

### 3.2 Beneficial Ownership Identification

Beneficial owners of legal entity customers shall be identified per the CDD Final Rule.

**Ownership Requirement:**
- Identify each individual who owns 25% or more (directly or indirectly) of the legal entity
- Collect name, date of birth, address, and identification number for each beneficial owner
- Verify beneficial owner identity using risk-based procedures

**Control Requirement:**
- Identify at least one individual with significant responsibility to control or manage the legal entity
- This may be a senior officer, manager, or other appropriate individual

**Beneficial Ownership Form:**
- Collect certification from legal entity customer identifying beneficial owners
- Retain beneficial ownership form and verification records
- Update beneficial ownership when changes are known

**Exemptions:**
- Exemptions apply only to specific entity types per CDD Final Rule
- Exemption basis is documented
- Exempted entities: publicly traded companies, regulated entities, government entities, etc.

### 3.3 Risk Assessment and Categorization

Customers shall be risk-rated based on money laundering and terrorist financing risk factors.

**Risk Factors:**
- Customer type (individual, legal entity, non-profit)
- Products and services used
- Geographic location (customer and transaction)
- Industry or business type
- Transaction patterns and volume
- Negative news or adverse information
- PEP status

**Risk Categories:**
- **High Risk:** Enhanced due diligence required, frequent monitoring, senior approval for relationship
- **Medium Risk:** Standard due diligence, periodic review
- **Low Risk:** Simplified due diligence where permitted

**Risk Rating Process:**
- Initial risk rating at account opening
- Risk rating reviewed when significant changes occur
- Periodic re-rating per risk level
- Risk rating documented and retained

### 3.4 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

Enhanced due diligence shall be performed for high-risk customers.

**EDD Triggers:**
- High-risk rating based on risk assessment
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
- Customers from high-risk jurisdictions
- Complex ownership structures
- Unusual business models
- Adverse media or negative information

**EDD Requirements:**
- Additional information gathering about customer, business, and source of funds
- Senior management approval for relationship
- More frequent monitoring and review
- Documentation of EDD performed
- Enhanced transaction monitoring

**High-Risk Jurisdiction Considerations:**
- FATF high-risk and increased monitoring lists
- U.S. government advisories
- FinCEN geographic targeting orders
- Country-specific risk assessments

### 3.5 Ongoing Customer Due Diligence

Customer information and risk shall be monitored on an ongoing basis.

**Ongoing Monitoring Requirements:**
- Update customer information when changes are known
- Periodic review based on risk level:
  - High Risk: Annual review
  - Medium Risk: Review every **[Number, e.g., 2]** years
  - Low Risk: Review every **[Number, e.g., 3]** years
- Transaction monitoring for unusual activity
- Periodic rescreening against sanction lists

**Trigger Events:**
- Significant change in customer activity
- Negative news or adverse information
- Customer information update
- SAR filing on customer
- Law enforcement inquiry

**Documentation:**
- Document periodic reviews
- Document customer information updates
- Document risk rating changes
- Retain records per BSA requirements

### 3.6 Sanctions and Watchlist Screening

Customers shall be screened against relevant sanctions and watchlists.

**Screening Requirements:**
- Screen at account opening
- Screen periodically for existing customers
- Screen when lists are updated
- Screen for transactions as appropriate

**Lists Screened:**
- OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) list
- OFAC Consolidated Sanctions List
- Other relevant lists per FIN-POL-003

**Match Handling:**
- Potential matches reviewed by trained staff
- True matches escalated to BSA/AML Officer
- Accounts blocked/rejected as required
- Documentation of screening and match disposition

### 3.7 Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)

Enhanced due diligence shall be applied to PEPs.

**PEP Definition:**
- Current or former senior foreign political figure
- Immediate family members of such persons
- Close associates of such persons

**PEP Requirements:**
- Screen for PEP status at onboarding
- Apply enhanced due diligence to identified PEPs
- Obtain senior management approval for PEP relationships
- Determine source of wealth and source of funds
- Enhanced ongoing monitoring

**Domestic PEPs:**
- Risk-based approach for domestic politically exposed persons
- Document risk assessment and controls applied

### 3.8 High-Risk Customer Types

Specific customer types require additional due diligence.

**Money Services Businesses (MSBs):**
- Verify MSB registration with FinCEN
- Understand MSB's AML program
- Obtain license information for state-licensed activities
- Enhanced transaction monitoring

**Foreign Financial Institutions:**
- Due diligence on correspondent relationships
- Understand institution's AML controls
- Prohibited relationships per Section 311

**Non-Profit Organizations:**
- Understand organization's purpose and activities
- Verify charitable status
- Enhanced due diligence for high-risk non-profits

**Cash-Intensive Businesses:**
- Understand expected transaction activity
- Enhanced monitoring for unusual patterns
- Document source of funds information

### 3.9 Customer Information Maintenance

Customer information shall be accurate and current.

**Information Updates:**
- Customers are encouraged to provide updated information
- Information verified when significant transactions occur
- Periodic verification based on risk level
- Process for customers to update their information

**Record Retention:**
- CIP records retained 5 years after account closure
- Beneficial ownership records retained 5 years after account closure
- Transaction records retained 5 years
- Records retrievable for regulatory examination

### 3.10 Account Restrictions and Closures

Accounts may be restricted or closed based on CDD findings.

**Restriction Triggers:**
- Inability to verify customer identity
- Significant negative information
- Sanctions match
- Excessive suspicious activity
- Customer non-cooperation

**Closure Process:**
- Decision to close documented
- Customer notified per legal requirements
- Regulatory filings completed (SAR if applicable)
- Funds handled per regulatory guidance
- Records retained post-closure

## 5. Definitions

| **Term** | **Definition** |
|---|---|
| **Beneficial Owner** | An individual who owns 25% or more of a legal entity, or an individual with significant control over the entity. |
| **Customer Due Diligence (CDD)** | The process of identifying and verifying customers, understanding the nature of customer relationships, and monitoring for suspicious activity. |
| **Customer Identification Program (CIP)** | The program required by the USA PATRIOT Act for verifying the identity of customers opening new accounts. |
| **Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)** | Additional scrutiny applied to higher-risk customers, requiring more information gathering and approval. |
| **Know Your Customer (KYC)** | The overall process of understanding customer identity, business purpose, and risk level. |
| **Politically Exposed Person (PEP)** | A current or former senior political figure, their family members, and close associates. |
| **Simplified Due Diligence** | Reduced due diligence permitted for low-risk customers where regulations allow. |
